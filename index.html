Framework · 02
07 / 16
You don't need hours. You need value.

Content methods.

Content doesn't have to be long. It has to be valuable.
01 · Text
Short post
Even 100 words counts. Keeps your profile active, keeps you in feeds.
02 · Audio
Voice recording
Write a brief script, record on your phone, transcribe, refine with AI. Half an hour of speaking = thousands of words to work from.
03 · Video
Short video
30 seconds to 1 minute. Face-to-camera or a voice-over works fine.
04 · Visual
Infographic
Use data you already have. A simple chart or diagram beats another text post.
05 · Data
Data post
A single insight or stat with context. One number, one sentence, one takeaway.

You don't have to write to grow

Growing through engagement.

The bare minimum
on LinkedIn is to engage.
That's your in.
01
Find the key voices in your niche
Who's already doing the thinking publicly? Follow them.
02
Identify relevant communities
The orgs and groups putting out content you care about.
03
Study your algorithm feed
It's already curated to you. Read what it's showing.
04
Analyse what people engage with
Which hooks land? Which topics? Which formats?
05
Engage thoughtfully
Comment, like, repost, add your perspective. Be a voice, not just a reader.
Discipline · Five questions
09 / 16
Ask yourself these. Every time.

Before you write.

01
Who do I want to read this?
Think of 2–3 specific people. What do they do? What do they care about? What are their pain points?
02
What am I adding that's new?
A perspective, a stat, a development, a challenge to a common assumption. If nothing new — don't write it.
03
Why should anyone care?
Add context. Hook it to what's happening in the world right now.
04
Would I actually read this?
If the answer is no — start again.
05
How can I make this genuinely valuable?
Insights, data, opportunities, edge. Content that gets saved and shared gives the reader something they can use.

01
Post on Tuesday, Wednesday or Thursday.
Do ✓
02
Post outbound links in your content. LinkedIn penalises this. Use LinkedIn Articles instead — keep people on-platform.
Don't ✗
03
Post 8–10am, around 1pm, or 4–6pm.
Do ✓
04
Use hashtags. Not worth it on LinkedIn. They don't meaningfully move reach.
Don't ✗
05
Write in plain English.
Do ✓
06
Use your own perspective, even as a beginner.
Do ✓
07
Write articles over 1,000 words. Be tight and concise. Shorter almost always outperforms.
Don't ✗
08
Track your analytics and engagement stats.
Do ✓
09
Copy what others are doing for inspiration. Get inspired. Never copy. Always create from scratch.
Don't ✗

Use AI to polish. Claude for writing and ideas. Perplexity for research — it cites sources.

Patterns we see across PD profiles

Profile improvement.

01
Engage more frequently in your niche.
Be present at every touchpoint. Consistency compounds.
02
Update your metadata.
Bio, header image, profile photo. Fill in every field — none of them are decorative.
03
Engage with PD content in the first hour.
Comment and repost within 60 minutes. PD has a large audience — that's free reach for you.
04
Post or engage 1–2× per week.
Consistency beats volume. A steady cadence trains the algorithm — and your audience.
