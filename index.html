Pre-Writing
Checklist
Before You Write
Ask Yourself These
01
Who do I want to read this?
Think of 2–3 specific people. What do they do? What do they care about? What are their pain points?
02
What am I adding that is new?
A new perspective, stat, or challenge to a common assumption. If you cannot add something new, it is not worth writing.
03
Why should anyone care?
Add context. Hook it to what is happening in the world right now. Make it timely and relevant.
04
Would I actually read this?
Be honest. If the answer is no — start again. Do not post for the sake of posting.
05
How can I make this genuinely valuable?
Think: insights, data points, opportunities, edge. Content that gets saved and shared gives readers something they can actually use.

Hook Framework
Content Hook Framework
The Curiosity
Gap — and
why it works.
The space between what your reader knows and what they want to know. Master this and your hook writes itself.
Speaker NoteThe curiosity gap is the psychological engine behind every high-performing LinkedIn post.

26
Hook
Framework
Creating a Hook
Work Backwards
Start at the end. Solution → Problem → Person.
①
Solution — Start here
What value, insight, or outcome are you offering?
Be specific. "How to write better" is vague. "How I cut my editing time in half using one prompt" is a solution.
②
Problem — Then this
What challenge does your solution directly address?
The problem your reader recognises as their own. Name it clearly and they'll feel seen.
③
Person — Finally, who
Who has this problem? The more specific, the better.
Your hook teases the solution just enough to create a gap. The reader needs to keep reading to close it.
